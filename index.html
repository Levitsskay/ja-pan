NAGOYA

221
50

Nagoya is the capital of the Japanese prefecture of Aichi, a large industrial and port city in the central part of the island of Honshu. The urban area of Naka is famous for its museums and pachinko (slot machine) halls. The entertainment district of Sakae is also located here, where tourists are attracted by the "Sky Boat" Ferris wheel built into the building of the shopping center. To the north of Naka is Nagoya Castle, a partially restored 1612 shogun residence that houses artifacts from the Edo period.

Niigata is a city determined by the decree of the Japanese government, the administrative center of Niigata Prefecture. Founded April 1, 1889. The area of the city is 726.10 km², the population is 808 189 people, the population density is 1113.05 people / km². Niigata is a large port on the coast of the Sea of Japan.

OSAKA

1121
398

Osaka is a major port and commercial city on the island of the Japanese archipelago of Honshu, famous for its modern architecture, nightlife, cafes and eateries. The main historical attraction of the city is the samurai castle of the 16th century, which has undergone several reconstructions. It is surrounded by a moat and a park where plums, peaches and sakura trees grow. Founded in the 8th century, the Sumiyoshi-taisha Shrine is one of the oldest in Japan.

Saitama is a city in Japan, the administrative center of Saitama Prefecture. The city is part of the metropolitan area of Greater Tokyo. The area of the city is 217.49 km², the population is 1 250 928 people, the population density is 5751.66 people / km².

UENO

1821
258

Ueno is a traditional area with graceful Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples. On an island in Ueno Park, there is a temple of the goddess Benzanten, built in the 17th century. At the Tokyo National Museum, you can see old Japanese works of art, and at the National Museum of Western Art, you can see the works of Vincent Van Gogh and Pablo Picasso. The park also houses the Ueno Zoo, home to the famous giant panda Xiang-Xiang. The spacious open market of Ameya Yokoch offers groceries and handicrafts.
